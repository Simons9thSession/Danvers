Hey I just found out about this place called Kings Park Psyche Center on Long Island, and I paid a visit to its many buildings. Here is one of the first of many buildings that are on the massive campus:

"Pictured above is Building 22 and out of frame to its left is Building 21."

This building has been standing there in place since it had been constructed in 1955, towards the end of the institutions life most patients from the other many residences on campus were brought here. Post closure the state commissioned a report in an effort to determine if anything was worth preserving. Unfortunately this building did not make the cut due to the fact that it does "not contribute, historically, or aesthetically to the campus". Luckily due to the cluttered nature of bureaucracy, these buildings will most likely outlive us.

"A picture of Building 22 sometime in the early to late '90s."

The building was a cool explore during the SEVEN whole hours I spent there, and I don't think I saw everything that there is to see. I'll post more photos later tonight.
